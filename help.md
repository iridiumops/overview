# Help & FAQ

**Q: What is an overview window?**

A: Internet spaceships are serious business and so are overviews. The overview window is the most important part of EVE Online user interface, yet the hardest thing to configure correctly - your ship's life depends on it.

> The overview window is one of the most essential components of EVE's user interface and is the primary means of viewing items in space.... Though it can be time-consuming to configure, the flexibility and effectiveness of a properly-configured overview justifies the time spent.
—— EVE University

The overview window is one of the most essential components of EVE's user interface and is the primary means of viewing items in space, both on local grid and in the star system. It displays ships, structures, and other entities with customizable tabs that filter different types of objects based on standings, types, and other criteria while displaying only the information that you need. A properly configured overview is essential for orienting yourself on grid, identifying threats and allies, completing your objectives, surviving unexpected and complex PvP situations.

**Q: What is an overview pack?**

A: Overview pack is a simple player-made UI mod for Eve Online. It is a collection of settings, filters and preconfigured tabs.

Though not all overview packs are made equal - which one to use will depend on your play style and preferences. Iridium Overview is one of such player made overview packs, perhaps the best. For over 10 years, Iridium Overview provided frequent and timely updates, earning trust of hundreds of players

**Q: Why not use the default?**

A: The default overview is going to get your shiny space ship killed. But you are in luck! There are multiple different overview packs, maintained by veteran players, that will help you get good overview setup without having to deal with overcomplicated settings and configuration and hoping you set it up right, so you don't confuse your corpmate with an NPC pirate. Ok, I am exaggerating a little - over time default overview received multitude of updates making it good enough for many, but player made overview packs like Iridium Overview still have countless advantages.

**Q: What version should I use?**

A: Always the latest version. It is available in the ingame chat channel "Iridium Overview" and on github release page.

**Q: Why use more than one tab?**

A: The amount of things in space would be often overwhelming, which is why preconfigured tabs filter objects down to more manageable amount based on their category and importance. Each tab serves different purpose based on the filter loaded. You might get by with just one tab, but having specific tabs for different situations, tasks and activities is extremely useful.

**Q: What is Iridium Overview?**

A: Iridium Overview is a player-made overview pack for EVE Online that provides a pre-configured set of 12 tabs, 38 overview presets, 3 bracket presets, and 3 d-scan presets which is the perfect balance between large complicated and too simplistic overview packs. Alternative filters provide easy yet powerful customization for your play-style. It's designed for every EVE player, whether newbie or veteran, and is useful for fleet and solo PvP, ratting, mining, looting, exploration, and all types of space.

**Q: What are brackets?**

A: Brackets are the visual symbols/icons that you see in space - outside of the overview itself. They help you quickly identify what's on grid without looking at the overview. Bracket filters control what brackets you can see in space, just like overview filters control what you see on the overview. Each overview tab can use different filter and different backet settings.

**Q: What is dscan?**

A: dscan, d-scan or directional scanner is a tool that allows you to get some limited information about ships and objects beyond current grid, making it essential early warning against danger or a tool to find targets. Unlike the overview window, dscan does not update automatically and must be triggered manually. Any filter can be used with dscan, but a dedicated d-scan filter provides best experience.

**Q: What does standing mean in this context?**

A: Standing refers to your relationship with other players or corporations, ranging from -10 (hostile, red) to +10 (friendly, green and blue). Iridium Overview uses standings to categorize objects and color-code them. For example on most tabs you don't want to see friendly ships while you want hostile players to unmistakably pop out among noise. 

**Q: What standings and states are there?**

A: iridium overview uses the following terms:
- All: Any standings, any special state
- Red: Negative standing (-5, -10)
- Neutral: Neutral standing or no standings
- Friendly: Positive standing (+5, +10), fleet, corporation or alliance members
- War targets: Valid hisec target due to Concord sanctioned war
- Hisec criminals: Valid target due to suspect/criminal timer or low security status

**Q: Do I need to reset my overview before installing?**

A: It's highly recommended to reset your old overview settings before installing Iridium Overview to avoid conflicts with other overview packs or older versions of the same pack. But you are free to combine overview packs if you have the need.

**Q: What is "Carbon Mode"?**

A: Carbon Mode is a single-window compact settings preset, designed for multiboxing and for small screen resolutions. The name refers to previous iteration of EVE UI which was sometimes called Carbon and which was limited to single overview window, unlike current EVE UI known as Photon that allows for multiple windows.

**Q: What are "Tab Icons"?**

A: Optional setting that replaces tab names with symbols. They are more compact and some players prefer graphical symbols to written text.

**Q: What is "PVX"?**

A: It is a term for player vs anything. Many older specialized filters for PVP (player vs player) and PVE (player vs environment and NPCs) were consolidated into universal pvx filters suitable for all situations and activities.

**Q: Which tab and filter should I use?**

A: Probably the main PVP or PVE tab. But check the list of filters [list of filters](parts/readme.md) to see what options are there for your activity.

**Q: What filters are provided by this overview pack?**

A: [List of filters](parts/readme.md) has detailed descriptions for each.

**Q: What if I don't like Iridium Overview?**

A: No problem! You can try other overview packs from the in-game `EVE Overviews` channel. Popular alternatives include *Z-S Overview*, *Kisover* and *Signal Cartel Overview Pack*. Or you can just reset your overview back to default. The overview settings also contain history of last 15 overview packs that you have loaded.

**Q: How to change what I see on overview?**

A: you can load different overview filter by right-clicking any tab and selecting the filter from the list of custom filters.

**Q: How to change what I see in space?**

A: you can load different bracket filter by right-clicking any tab and selecting the bracket filter from the list of custom filters. Any filter can be used as bracket filter, but use of dedicated bracket filters is recommended.

**Q: How to add colors to my custom filter names?**

A: Filter name colors are not fully supported by the game client and require exporting the overview to a file, editing it there and the reimporting. It is not hard thing to do, but also not worth the effort.

**Q: How to move tab to new window?**

A: Right-click the tab in question and select move to new window.

**Q: Can I reorder the tabs?**

A: No, not easily. You can however swap the tabs filters, states settings and names in the settings.

**Q: Can I change the columns?**

A: Yes, each tab can have different columns visible, have them in different order and size. The options are in the right click menu on the tab under columns. Tick the ones you want or select change order and drag them up and down.

**Q: What layout is recommended?**

A: You can have any layout you want. I would suggest starting with two windows - one for things on local grid (tabs PVE, PVP, Loot, etc.) and second for system navigation (System and Warp tabs). Then perhaps experiment with having Drones and Ships in their own separate window.

**Q: Why so many tabs?**

A: Iridium overview is all about giving you options. Yes, you probably wont need for example both Ships and NPC tabs or Fleet and Friendly tab at the same time, so pick the option you prefer and delete the other.

**Q: Does it matter what order I click the install links in?**

A: No, currently is does not matter.

**Q: Why are there 3 install links to click? Other overviews have less/more links!**

A: Due to game limitations each link can install maximum of 16 filters. To install all the filters provided by iridium overview 3 links are needed.

**Q: Does it update automatically when new version is released?**

A: No, the game does not support that - you have to update the overview pack manually by clicking the install links again.

**Q: Will updating the overview break my custom changes?**

A: Filters with the same name will be overwritten, most other settings are preserved, but nothing is guaranteed. If you made significant changes that you care about, always have backups of your client profile or at least export your custom changes to a YAML file.

**Q: I am part of nullsec alliance, can I use the overview pack?**

A: Yes, the overview pack has sensible settings common to practically all groups.

**Q: I am part of NPSI fleet, can I use the overview pack?**

A: Yes, pick a tab or load a filter that shows all ships regardless of standings and you are good to go.

**Q: I am part of Faction Warfare or Pirate Faction, can I use the overview pack?**

A: Yes, just beware of awoxers.

**Q: I am part of NPC Corp, can I use the overview pack?**

A: Yes, NPC corporation members are correctly treated like neutrals.

**Q: Can you remove NPC drones from abyss? They clutter the overview!**

A: No, because they share the same group ID as other NPCs that you do need to destroy, unlike the drones. Please complain to CCP to split them logically.

**Q: Can you add beacons for [some specific site] to the overview?**

A: No, all beacons have the same group ID, I cannot distinguish between them.

**Q: Can you filter upwell structures by their size?**

A: No, structures are illogically grouped by their function not their size. Please complain to CCP instead.

**Q: Can I use different overview on my other character?**

A: No, overview settings is per account and shared between all 3 characters.

**Q: I don't want pods/capsules on my overview. Pods are not ships!**

A: Alright, many filters have variation without pods - look for filters that have "-capsules" in the name and use those instead.

**Q: I don't want NPCs/rats on my overview, only players!**

A: Alright, many filters have variation without NPCs - look for filters that have "-npc" in the name and use those instead.

**Q: How to copy overview settings to my other characters?**

A: Characters on the same account already share the overview settings. To share the overview with characters on other accounts you need 3rd party tools like [EANM](https://github.com/FontaineRiant/EANM) which can copy the entire client settings, overview included.

**Q: My tabs are suddenly very tall, help!?**

A: You have encountered a common bug. It is not unique to Iridium Overview. Just log out and log back in, it will be normal again.

**Q: What columns should I use?**

A: Icon, Distance and Type are essential for every tab and situation. Name, corp and alliance tickers are useful. Streamers often hide these to maintain opsec. Velocity is very important for pvp and even some harder pve. Angular velocity will tell you if your large slow guns can track the ship orbiting you. Alternatively traversal velocity can be used instead of angular velocity for the same purpose on smaller more nimble ships. You need to be aware of your relative movement, but in exchange it will tell you how well you matched the traversal with it. Size and radial velocity are laregly useless. Tag can be useful when cooperating with other players as part of small well organized fleet, but is mostly unused by players with the exception of incursion content and some select wormhole sites.
